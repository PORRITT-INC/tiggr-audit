The Problem We Sell Into

Regulated buyers are told to adopt frontier AI — with no record they control.

Enterprises in regulated sectors are being pushed to adopt frontier AI while having no provider-independent record of what their staff sent to a model or got back. Provider-side consoles are admin-gated, cloud-resident, and disappear if access does. Provider access can change overnight — and your audit trail shouldn’t live only on their side. DOE, national labs, defense, finance, and healthcare increasingly need to prove what their AI workflow did, on their own infrastructure.

What TIGGR Is

A local HTTPS proxy between your AI client and the provider endpoint.

It sits between the AI client (Claude Desktop / Cowork, or any API client) and the provider endpoint. For every request and response, it:

1

Computes a SHA-256 hash and writes the full exchange to a local SQLite log.

2

Links each record into an append-only hash chain — altering any past record breaks the chain at that point.

3

Ships an offline verification CLI (tiggr verify) that walks the chain, flags the first broken link, and exports to JSON/JSONL for auditors.

4

Uses a customer-controlled local root CA; the log never leaves the machine; the API key is redacted at rest.

Tier-1 MVP is built and running today. Tier 2 (schema validation, anomaly bands, dashboard) and Tier 3 (federated cross-machine attestation) are roadmapped.
